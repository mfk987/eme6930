Education & Related Experience

Berry College

Degree: Bachelor of Science in Environmental Science & Geology; Minor in Economics

Years Attended: 2006 - 2009

Melissa cultivated her love of the outdoors and environmental science at Berry College's 24,000+ acre campus in northwest Georgia. With opportunities to monitor groundwater levels for over one year, study man-made lake restoration, gather soil samples from local caves, study stream flow and brownfield restoration Melissa gained years of hands-on field experience.

In the Summer of 2008, Melissa was able to study abroad in Iceland for two weeks and see many of the geologic features from textbooks in real life. The highlight of the trip was walking on a glacier, then visiting the black sand beaches and ending the day visiting a thermal hot sping to relax.

Duke University

Degree: Master of Environmental Management; Certificate in Geospatial Analysis

Years Attended: 2010 - 2012

Her studies were continued at Duke University's Nicholas School of the Environment where Melissa's studies focused on coastal environmental science and geospatial analysis. Her Master's Project focused on projecting sea level rise in three Louisana coastal parishes and developing policy recommendations to mitigate the effects on people and property.

In the spring of 2012, Melissa was able to study abroad in the Brittany region of France focusing on the sustainable activities and relationship the people have with the land. The highligh of the trip was eathing crepes and drinking cider for breakfast, lunch and dinner!

University of South Florida

Degree: Master of Science in Learning Design & Technology; Certificate in Web Design

Years Attended: 2019 - 2021

After teaching middle school science for two years and working at MetLife for four years, Melissa decided to continue her studies in the Learning Design and Technology program at USF.

Prior experience with instructional design included developing lessons for 6th, 7th and 8th grade students and later planning and conducting professional training as a training subject matter expert for her current department.
